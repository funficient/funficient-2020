Bodystorming


Duration: 2 - 5 hours

Validate assumptions early with bodystorming.

Bodystorming is perfect if you are looking for opportunities. An interactive and fun role-playing activity, it enables you to gather insights by doing, rather than talking or thinking, removing assumptions from the word go.

Placestorming

Duration: 2 - 3 hours

Originally designed by game designer Jane McGonigal, placestorming is a fully immersive game experience to discover new ideas.

Placestorming gets participants physically active in real environments such as coffee shops, telephone booths or libraries.  The teams use props while visiting specific locations to accomplish set-out missions. 

They gain insights on their playful discovery while physically enacting possible technological innovations.

What to expect

Contact us to book an introductory call to tell us the problem you want to solve and pinpoint your main objective(s).  Based on your budget, time, location and availability we'll design a workshop just for you (1 - 2 weeks lead time).  If any preparation is required we'll let you know in advance, but in principle we believe that each session should be a self-contained unit where all planning, execution and reporting are done in the session.

On the day of the workshop we'll facilitate the session and adapt the plan as required and based on participant needs.  It often happens that once we get into the details what was originally thought to be the main problem turns out not to be the problem at all, requiring the planned workshop to change into the new direction. 

The session starts with a warmer and introductions, followed by the different activities and finally a retrospective to review the findings and agree on the next steps.

Frequently asked questions

  • Why use playful design over existing design thinking methods?
    Playful design is not different to design thinking and doesn't replace the existing methods that is often used.  It rather ads an element of imagination and lateral thinking to the existing methods.  Many of the games are in fact well known design thinking tools.
  • Who is it for?
    Product discovery is suitable for any potential new project to identify and do some initial research before committing to a project to lower the risk and the cost.  Entrepreneurs looking for a startup idea, digital agencies looking for original ideas or corporate companies in need of product growth or innovation can benefit from playful discovery.

    It is ideal for anyone looking for a new idea or pivoting an existing idea. 
  •  How many people can play?
    We believe that less is more and like to focus on small groups. The ideal size is 5 - 6 people per team with a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 8 per group with a maximum of 5 teams per session.  The larger the group the more time is required thus it is more efficient to have small focus groups.

    Depending on the organization we need representatives from all the key functions such as design, development, finance, customer communications and support.
  • What is playful design?
    Playful design uses well known game mechanics such as time limits, randomness and the use of avatars; movement and imagination to augment traditional design.
