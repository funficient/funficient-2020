New Possibilities

The story of funficient and a vision for teal workplaces.

Vision

To create high performing, happy teams that build awesome products and services that contribute to positive change in the world.

How funficient was born.

Tired of leaving my soul at the door in the name of professionalism each time I clocked in for work, and sick of meetings for the sake of meetings, I yearned for fun and meaning in the work I do.  I yearned to think more, do more, be more.

Remembering my first job at a startup where we spent more time chatting and playing than doing actual work, yet being more productive than  highly structured workplaces at large corporations, I knew that a bit of fun was the missing ingredient to create more productive and strong teams. After all, relationships are the glue that bonds teams together.

A journey to put the fun back into efficiency lead me across the globe in search of productive, innovative and happy teams.  The search continues, and I'll continue to share my learnings for those with the same vision of the future of work and teal organizations.

Teal principles.

01.

Movement matters

A body in motion stays in motion.  We inject movement in processes, projects and people to ensure fresh thinking and engagement.

02.

Aim for equality

No two people are the same, but everyone is equally valuable.  The goal is to ensure we optimise the value by ensuring each person is in the right place at the right time.

03.

Never stop learning

Everything is possible as long as you ask the right questions and keep learning something.  Build brain agility and get comfortable with the uncomfortable.

04.

Relationships matter

Software development is a team sport. Maintaining healthy relationships are more important than being a sole genius in the corner.

05.

No judgment

Nothing's off the table for discussion and no idea is bad. Express your individuality and include others without judgment.

06.

Win-Win solutions

If everyone doesn't win, everyone loses in the end. Negotiate, don't compromise. Focus on similarities and mutual goals, not differences.

Want to take your team to the next level?

Time for teal

Where agile and lean focus on process efficiency, teal adds another dimension to focus on the human aspect and organizations as an ecosystem.

Teal is the next level in organizational development.  Some more major differences compared to the majority of organizations running Scrum are that it replaces management with coaching, it introduces conflict resolution processes and decentralize decision making to team level.
