New Possibilities

The story of funficient and a vision for teal workplaces.

Vision

To create high performing, happy teams that build awesome products and services that contribute to positive change in the world.

What makes us happy is social connection, meaningful work and the ability to get better at our craft.

So why not provide this at work?

Tired of leaving my soul at the door in the name of professionalism each time I clocked in for work, I yearned for fun and meaning in the work I do.  I yearned to think more, do more, be more.

Remembering my first job at a startup where we spent more time talking and playing than doing actual work, yet ended up more productive than the highly structured workplaces at large corporations, I knew that a bit of fun was the missing ingredient to create strong and more productive. After all, relationships are the glue that bonds people together.  Nothing else matters more.

A journey to put the fun back into efficiency lead me across the globe in search of productive, innovative and happy teams.  What makes teams and workplaces great?  How do these teams build products? And how do we put it all together?  The result of this ongoing work is funficient, with a goal to put the fun back into efficiency.

Want to take your team to the next level?

Time for teal

Where agile and lean focus on process efficiency, teal adds a dimension to focus on the human aspect and organizations as an ecosystem, as part of the bigger world and the need becomes to maintain equilibrium rather than efficiency in one area.

Teal is the next level in organizational development.  Some more major differences compared to the majority of organizations running Scrum are that it replaces management with coaching, it introduces conflict resolution processes and decentralize decision making to team level.

Read more about teal organizations here.

Teal principles.

1

Movement matters

A body in motion stays in motion. We inject movement in processes, projects and people to ensure fresh thinking and higher engagement.
2

Aim for equality

No two people are the same, but everyone is equally valuable. The goal is to ensure we optimise value by ensuring each person is in the right place at the right time, doing the right thing, right.
3

Never stop learning

Everything is possible as long as you ask the right questions and keep learning something. Build brain agility and get comfortable with the uncomfortable.
4

Relationships matter

Software development is a team sport. Maintaining and building healthy relationships are more important than being a sole genius in the corner.
5

No judgment

Nothing's off the table for discussion and no idea is bad. Express your individuality and include others without judgment. You can't relax and have fun if you worry about being judged.
6

Find win-win solutions

If everyone doesn't win, everyone loses in the end. Negotiate, don't compromise. Focus on similarities and mutual goals, not differences to find the intersection that helps everyone get what they want.
